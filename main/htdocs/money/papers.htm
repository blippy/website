52-week high and momentum investing (PDF) - the 52-week high price explains a large portion of the profits from momentum investing - results indicate that the 52-week measure has predictive power whether or not individual stocks have had extreme past returns. This suggests that price level is important, and is consistent with an anchor-and-adjust bias. After controlling for the size effect and the impact of bid-ask bounce, returns associated with winners and losers identified by the 52-week high strategy are about twice as large as those associated with the other strategies.

High Yield, Low Payout
Patel et al. 2006.
The authors tool a two-stage process of first creating three dividend yield baskets by yield, then within each of these three baskets, categorising stocks by payout ratio: low, medium, and high. They concluded that the high yield low payout stocks performed best, and beat the S&500 by a wide margin.

Shorted stocks - Over the last decade, buying heavily shorted large and mid-cap stocks (the top two quartiles of all shortable stocks by market capitalization) would have beaten the S&P 500 by 9.28 per cent each and every year. Market cap matters - short sellers tend to be right about smaller names, with micro-caps delivering negative returns when the same strategy was used. Added 01-Jan-2013

Studying different Systematic Value Investing Strategies on the Eurozone stock market
Vanstraceele and Allaeys, 2010
Covers period 16-Jun-1999 to 13-Jun-2009. It evaluates the value-based strategies: Magic Formula, Piotroski, NCAV and ERP5. It concludes that value-based strategies work, and that combining them works best.

Surprise! Higher Dividends = Higher Earnings Growth
Arnott and Clifford, 2003.
Contrary to popular belief, EP growth is fastest when current dividend payouts are high in relation to earnings.
