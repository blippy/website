Neff stock filter

This filter was devised by John Neff - a value investor for the Windsor fund. I don't think he runs his filter mechanically, so the results of the filter should probably considered as interesting tips rather than a strategy to follow blindly.

Buy Criteria

Neff is interested in companies where: 
          (total return)/ PE > 2
where he defines 
          total return = sustainable earnings growth rate + dividend yield
He further states that a reasonable and sustainable earnings growth rate between 7% and 20% usually caught investors' attention. He is suspicious of growth rates above 20%, which he claims involves too much risk.

Sell Criteria

Unknown - but presumably shares are to be sold when they no longer meet value criteria.

Source

Found on a page at The Motley Fool. I think the information is derived from "John Neff on Investing" by John Neff and Steven Mintz.
