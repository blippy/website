Simplest way

This formula apparently appeared in Medical Economics 20-Sep-1976, titled "The Simplest Way to Select Bargain Stocks". Graham was 82 at the time. Selection Criteria:
  1. Cheapness: stock having an earning yield (inverse of PER) that is at least twice as big as the average yield on long-term AAA corporate bonds, or 10%, whichever is greater
  2. Safety: leverage ratio <= 2. Leverage ratio is is the total assets to shareholder's equity.
Aim to build a portfolio of 30 stocks. You need to give the program a minimum of five years to work.

Selling criteria: two years or 50% profit, whichever came first.
