Title: 3 Quick Rules of Thumbs to Determine a Strong Business

  1. Operating Cash Flow - how much real cash the company generates from its core business
    This is the real cash that the business generates; after all the goods sold, expenses and inventory purchase. Note that the cost of inventory is often overlooked.
    Look for: At least 12% of the total capital invested. Rising consistently year over year
  2. Free cash flow - how profitable the company is after capital expenditure and loan repayments
    Take the operating cash flow and subtract capex, acquisitions, debt repayment and any dividend
    Look for: FCF positive at least 5 out of the last 10 years
  3. ROCE
    Divide operating profit by capital employed (quity + debt)
    Look for: ROCE > 10% consistently. Above 15% indicates a strong business.
